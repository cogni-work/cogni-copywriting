# Example Email - SCQA Framework

This example demonstrates a business email using the SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer) framework for persuasive communication.

---

**Subject:** Solution for Q4 Budget Shortfall - Marketing Reallocation Proposal

Hi Sarah,

**Situation:** We're tracking toward our Q4 revenue target of $2.4M with strong performance in enterprise accounts (110% of plan). Our SMB segment is running at 78% of plan due to competitive pressure from two new entrants.

**Complication:** Marketing allocated 60% of Q4 budget to SMB campaigns, but ROI is now 3.2:1 versus enterprise ROI of 8.5:1. If we maintain current allocation, we'll miss Q4 target by approximately $180K despite having high-performing channels available.

**Question:** Should we reallocate underperforming SMB budget to double down on enterprise momentum?

**Answer:** I recommend shifting $75K from SMB digital ads to enterprise account-based marketing and field events. This reallocation would:

- Increase projected enterprise revenue by $240K (based on current 8.5:1 ROI)
- Accelerate three pipeline opportunities currently at 85% close probability
- Maintain minimum viable SMB presence ($50K budget remaining)
- Close revenue gap and potentially exceed Q4 target by 2-3%

**Trade-offs:** We'd reduce SMB lead volume by ~200 leads, but our current conversion rate suggests only 6-8 would close this quarter anyway (~$24K revenue). The enterprise opportunity is substantially higher.

**Next Steps:** Can we discuss this Wednesday? I've prepared detailed analysis with account-specific projections and timeline for budget transfer. We'd need to move by Friday to capture the field event opportunity.

Let me know your availability. Happy to share the detailed analysis beforehand if helpful.

Best,
Marcus

---

## Analysis of This Example

**Framework Application:**
- **Situation:** Sets context with current performance data
- **Complication:** Introduces problem (misallocated budget, suboptimal ROI)
- **Question:** Frames decision clearly and explicitly
- **Answer:** Provides recommended solution with supporting evidence

**Quality Metrics:**
- **Flesch Reading Ease:** ~58 (standard difficulty)
- **Paragraph Length:** 2-4 sentences average
- **Word Count:** ~250 words (optimal for email)
- **Visual Elements:** Bold section headers, bullets for key benefits

**Deliverable Compliance:**
- **Length:** 5 paragraphs (within email best practice)
- **Subject Line:** Action-oriented with clear topic
- **Formality:** Medium-professional
- **Mobile-friendly:** Scannable structure with short paragraphs

**Why This Works:**

1. **Narrative structure:** SCQA builds natural story arc toward decision
2. **Quantitative evidence:** Uses specific numbers (110%, 78%, 8.5:1 ROI) for credibility
3. **Addresses objections:** Trade-offs section shows balanced thinking
4. **Clear ask:** Specific request (Wednesday meeting) with timeline urgency
5. **Respectful tone:** "Can we discuss" vs "We need to decide" maintains collaboration

**SCQA vs BLUF Choice:**

This email uses SCQA instead of BLUF because:
- **Situation requires context:** Reader needs performance data to understand problem
- **Recommendation may be controversial:** Narrative build creates persuasive case
- **Decision not yet made:** SCQA invites dialogue, BLUF announces decisions

For urgent or executive communication, BLUF would be more appropriate.

**Subject Line Analysis:**

"Solution for Q4 Budget Shortfall - Marketing Reallocation Proposal"
- Front-loads benefit ("Solution")
- Identifies topic clearly ("Q4 Budget Shortfall")
- Sets expectation ("Proposal" indicates request for decision)
- Length: 9 words (optimal for mobile preview)
