# Example Brief - Pyramid Principle

This example demonstrates an executive brief using the Pyramid Principle framework for structured analysis and recommendation.

---

# Customer Support Automation Strategy
**Executive Brief | October 29, 2025**

## Recommendation

Implement AI-powered tier-1 support automation by Q1 2026 to reduce support costs by 35% while improving response times by 60%. Total investment of $280K will deliver $450K annual savings and measurably improve customer satisfaction.

---

## Supporting Arguments

### 1. Current Support Model Is Unsustainable

Our support costs increased 47% year-over-year while customer satisfaction declined from 4.2 to 3.8 stars. Three factors drive this crisis:

- **Volume growth outpaces hiring:** Ticket volume grew 89% versus 23% headcount increase
- **Tier-1 tickets consume premium resources:** 68% of tickets are routine queries handled by senior agents at $65/hour
- **Response times damage retention:** Average wait time increased from 4 to 11 minutes; 18% of customers cite support delays as cancellation reason

### 2. AI Automation Delivers Proven ROI in Similar Context

Industry benchmarks and pilot data demonstrate viability:

**Industry Evidence:**
- Zendesk customers average 32% cost reduction with AI tier-1 automation
- Intercom reports 60% of tier-1 queries successfully automated
- Gartner predicts 80% of support interactions will use AI by 2025

**Our Pilot Results (2-month test, 500 customer sample):**
- 71% of tier-1 queries fully resolved by AI (exceeds 60% industry benchmark)
- Response time reduced from 11 minutes to 45 seconds
- Customer satisfaction improved from 3.8 to 4.3 stars for AI-handled tickets
- Zero escalation errors (proper routing to humans maintained)

### 3. Implementation Risk Is Manageable with Phased Approach

Conservative rollout mitigates typical AI deployment concerns:

**Phase 1 (Q1 2026):** Deploy to 25% of tier-1 traffic, maintain full human backup
**Phase 2 (Q2 2026):** Scale to 75% based on quality metrics validation
**Phase 3 (Q3 2026):** Full deployment with continuous monitoring

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Human escalation available within 30 seconds for all conversations
- Agent review of 100% of AI responses during Phase 1 (sampling in Phase 2-3)
- Customer opt-out option preserved ("speak to human agent")
- Gradual staff transition (training for tier-2 roles, not layoffs)

| Metric | Current State | Year 1 Post-Implementation | Year 2 |
|--------|--------------|---------------------------|--------|
| Annual support cost | $1.28M | $830K (-35%) | $780K (-39%) |
| Avg response time | 11 min | 4.5 min (-59%) | 3.2 min (-71%) |
| Customer satisfaction | 3.8 ★ | 4.3 ★ | 4.5 ★ |
| Implementation cost | - | $280K | $40K maintenance |

**Break-even:** Month 8 of Year 1
**3-year NPV:** $1.1M (assuming 8% discount rate)

## Next Steps

1. **Decision Required:** Approve $280K capital expenditure by November 15 for Q1 deployment
2. **Vendor Selection:** Evaluate Zendesk AI vs Intercom Fin vs custom build (4-week RFP)
3. **Team Preparation:** Begin tier-2 training program for 12 agents transitioning roles
4. **Success Metrics:** Establish baseline measurements and Phase 1 success criteria

**Decision Deadline:** Board approval needed by November 15 to meet Q1 2026 launch target.

## Analysis of This Example

**Framework Application:**
- **Top of Pyramid:** Recommendation stated immediately with quantified benefits
- **Supporting Layer 1:** Three main arguments (unsustainable model, proven ROI, manageable risk)
- **Supporting Layer 2:** Each argument backed by 3-5 specific evidence points
- **Conclusion:** Returns to action with clear next steps and timeline

**Quality Metrics:**
- **Flesch Reading Ease:** ~52 (appropriate difficulty for executive brief)
- **Paragraph Length:** 3-5 sentences average
- **Length:** ~500 words (fits 2 pages)
- **Visual Elements:** Table, headers, bullets, bold emphasis

**Deliverable Compliance:**
- **Length:** 2 pages (within brief guidelines)
- **Formality:** High (consulting-style language)
- **Structure:** Classic brief format with executive summary approach
- **Audience:** Executive decision-makers requiring structured analysis

**Why This Works:**

1. **Pyramid structure:** Main point first, then supporting arguments in logical order
2. **Quantitative rigor:** Specific metrics throughout (47%, $280K, 71%)
3. **Balanced analysis:** Acknowledges risks and provides mitigation strategy
4. **Actionable conclusion:** Clear decision request with deadline
5. **McKinsey-style clarity:** MECE arguments (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)

**Pyramid Principle Application:**

The brief follows Barbara Minto's Pyramid Principle:
- **Vertical logic:** Each level answers "Why?" of level above
- **Horizontal logic:** Arguments at same level are MECE and parallel
- **Inductive reasoning:** Multiple supporting points lead to single conclusion

**Document Hierarchy:**
```
Recommendation (Top)
├── Unsustainable Model (Supporting 1)
│   ├── Volume outpaces hiring
│   ├── Premium resources on routine tasks
│   └── Response times damage retention
├── Proven ROI (Supporting 2)
│   ├── Industry benchmarks
│   └── Pilot results
└── Manageable Risk (Supporting 3)
    ├── Phased approach
    └── Risk mitigation strategies
```

**Tone Analysis:**

- **Confident but not aggressive:** "Implement" vs "Consider implementing"
- **Evidence-based:** Every claim supported by data
- **Balanced:** Acknowledges risks and costs
- **Action-oriented:** Clear next steps and deadlines
