# Executive Impact

## Quick Reference

**Use when:** Writing for C-suite, board members, senior leadership, key stakeholders
**Core principle:** Executives decide; give them what they need in the format they expect
**Key techniques:** Lead with recommendation, quantify everything, respect time
**Target:** Decision-ready in 60 seconds of reading

## Executive Reading Behavior

Research on executive reading patterns:
- Average time spent: 30-60 seconds for first pass
- Reading pattern: Headlines, bullets, bold text, then selective deep-dive
- Decision driver: "What should I do?" before "Why?"
- Trust signal: Data and specificity over assertion

## The 5 Executive Imperatives

### 1. Lead with the Ask

Executives want the recommendation before the rationale.

**Anti-Pattern:**
> "After extensive analysis of market conditions, competitive positioning, and internal capabilities, our team has developed several options for consideration..."

**Executive Pattern:**
> "**Recommendation:** Approve $2.4M investment in Platform X.
>
> **Key rationale:** 18-month payback, 340% 3-year ROI, competitive necessity."

**Structure:**
1. Recommendation (what to do)
2. Key rationale (why, in 10 words or less)
3. Supporting detail (for those who want it)

### 2. Quantify Everything

Executives think in numbers. Translate all benefits to metrics.

**Weak Statements → Executive Statements:**

| Weak | Executive-Ready |
|------|----------------|
| "Improves efficiency" | "Reduces processing time by 60%" |
| "Saves money" | "Saves $400K annually" |
| "Better customer experience" | "Increases NPS from 42 to 67" |
| "Faster time to market" | "Launches 8 weeks earlier" |
| "Reduces risk" | "Reduces compliance exposure by $2.3M" |

**Triple-Quantify Key Benefits:**
- What: $400K savings
- How much: 23% of current spend
- Compared to: $100K investment = 4:1 return

### 3. Respect Time Scarcity

Every extra word is a cost. Ruthless brevity signals respect.

**Time Respect Principles:**

**One-Page Maximum:** If it can't fit on one page, it's not focused enough.

**30-Second Scannable:** Bold key points, use bullet lists, enable skimming.

**Front-Load Value:** First sentence of each paragraph = key point.

**Word Budget by Section:**
- Executive Summary: 100-150 words
- Recommendation: 1-2 sentences
- Background: 50-100 words (most executives skip)
- Analysis: 150-200 words (bullets preferred)
- Next Steps: 3-5 items, one line each

### 4. Provide Decision Clarity

Remove ambiguity from the decision they need to make.

**Decision Clarity Framework:**

**The Ask:** What specific action are you requesting?
- ❌ "Consider this proposal"
- ✅ "Approve Phase 1 funding of $500K"

**The Options:** If choices exist, make them clear
- Option A: [Description] — [Cost] — [Benefit] — [Risk]
- Option B: [Description] — [Cost] — [Benefit] — [Risk]
- **Recommended: Option A** — [One-line rationale]

**The Timeline:** When do they need to decide?
- ❌ "Please respond at your convenience"
- ✅ "Decision needed by March 15 for Q2 implementation"

**The Consequence:** What happens if they don't act?
- ❌ "This is important"
- ✅ "Delay past Q2 increases implementation cost by $200K"

### 5. Signal Credibility

Executives filter for signals of thoroughness and reliability.

**Credibility Signals:**

**Precision:** "$127,400" > "about $130K"
**Attribution:** "Per McKinsey's 2024 report" > "Research shows"
**Methodology:** "Based on 6-month pilot with 340 users" > "Testing indicates"
**Risk Acknowledgment:** "Key risk: vendor dependency" > Omitting risks

**Trust Destroyers:**
- Vague claims without data
- No mention of risks or challenges
- Unrealistic projections
- Internal jargon or acronyms

## Executive Document Templates

### Board Memo Structure

```markdown
**TO:** [Board/Executive Committee]
**FROM:** [Name, Title]
**DATE:** [Date]
**RE:** [Action Required: Topic]

## Recommendation
[One sentence: what you want them to do]

## Strategic Context
[2-3 sentences: why this matters now]

## Key Financials
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Investment Required | $X |
| Expected Return | $Y |
| Payback Period | Z months |
| Risk Exposure | $W |

## Options Considered
1. **Recommended:** [Option] — [Rationale]
2. **Alternative:** [Option] — [Why not chosen]
3. **Status Quo:** [Risks of inaction]

## Next Steps (if approved)
1. [Action] — [Owner] — [Date]
2. [Action] — [Owner] — [Date]

## Appendix
[Supporting detail for deep-dive]
```

### Executive Summary Structure

```markdown
## Executive Summary

**Bottom Line:** [One sentence recommendation/finding]

**Key Findings:**
- [Finding 1 with quantification]
- [Finding 2 with quantification]
- [Finding 3 with quantification]

**Recommended Action:** [Specific ask]

**Investment:** $X | **Return:** $Y | **Timeline:** Z months
```

### Decision Request Structure

```markdown
## Decision Required: [Topic]

**Request:** [Specific approval/decision needed]

**Deadline:** [Date and why]

**Context:** [2-3 sentences of relevant background]

**Recommendation:** [What you advise and why]

**Alternatives:**
- Option A: [Brief description] — **Recommended**
- Option B: [Brief description]
- Option C: [Brief description]

**Risk of Delay:** [Quantified consequence]
```

## Impact Techniques for Executives

### Number Plays (Executive Application)

**Always include:**
- ROI calculation
- Payback period
- Year-over-year comparison
- Industry benchmark comparison

**Format:**
> **ROI:** 340% over 3 years
> **Payback:** 18 months
> **vs. Industry:** 2.3x average performance
> **vs. Last Year:** 45% improvement

### Power Words (Executive Application)

**High-impact for executives:**
- Strategic, proven, validated, accelerated
- Revenue, margin, growth, efficiency
- Risk-adjusted, competitive, market-leading
- Scalable, sustainable, measurable

**Avoid with executives:**
- Revolutionary, game-changing, exciting (sounds like marketing)
- Unique, unprecedented (triggers skepticism)
- Synergy, leverage, ideate (corporate buzzword fatigue)

### Rhetorical Devices (Executive Application)

**Most effective:**
- Rule of Three (tricolon) for key points
- Antithesis for decision framing
- Short punchy closing sentences

**Example:**
> "Three priorities: reduce cost, improve speed, maintain quality.
> The question isn't whether we can afford this investment—it's whether we can afford not to make it.
> The time is now."

## Executive Impact Checklist

Before sending to executives:

- [ ] Recommendation in first 2 sentences
- [ ] All benefits quantified with numbers
- [ ] Decision/action clearly stated
- [ ] Timeline/deadline specified
- [ ] Fits on one page (or clear executive summary)
- [ ] 30-second scannable (bold, bullets, headers)
- [ ] Risks acknowledged
- [ ] Data sources cited
- [ ] No unexplained jargon or acronyms
- [ ] "So what?" answered for every point

## Common Executive Document Failures

| Failure | Impact | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Burying the lead | Lost attention before key point | Move recommendation to line 1 |
| Vague benefits | No basis for decision | Quantify every claim |
| Missing ask | Unclear what's needed | Explicit decision/action request |
| Too long | Won't be read completely | One page maximum |
| No timeline | Deprioritized | Specify deadline and consequence |
| Risk-free presentation | Lacks credibility | Acknowledge key risks |
