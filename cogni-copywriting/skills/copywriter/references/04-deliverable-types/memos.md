# Professional Memos

## Quick Reference
**Purpose:** Brief internal communication
**Length:** Typically 1 page
**Formality:** Medium (professional but conversational)
**Audience:** Internal colleagues/teams
**Best frameworks:** BLUF, Pyramid Principle, SCQA
**Key principles:** Clarity, conciseness, active voice

## Standard Structure

### Required Elements

1. **Header Block**
   ```
   TO: [Recipient name/department]
   FROM: [Your name/title]
   DATE: [Full date]
   SUBJECT: [Specific, descriptive subject line]
   ```

2. **Opening Paragraph** (BLUF or main point)
   - State purpose immediately
   - Use recommended framework (typically BLUF)
   - 2-4 sentences maximum

3. **Body Paragraphs** (Context and details)
   - Provide context and details
   - Use bullet points for lists
   - Keep paragraphs short (3-4 lines)
   - Target: 2-4 paragraphs

4. **Closing** (Action items)
   - Action items with deadlines
   - Next steps
   - Courteous close

## Recommended Framework Integration

### With BLUF (Most Common)
```markdown
TO: Marketing Team
FROM: Sarah Johnson, Marketing Director
DATE: October 29, 2025
SUBJECT: Q4 Campaign Launch Delayed to November 15

BLUF: We're delaying the Q4 campaign launch from November 1 to November 15 due to vendor delays. All other timelines remain unchanged.

Context: Our primary vendor experienced supply chain issues affecting creative asset delivery. They've committed to delivery by November 8, giving us one week for final review.

Impact:
- Holiday shopping season coverage reduced by 2 weeks
- Budget allocation remains the same
- Team bandwidth freed for year-end planning

Next Steps:
- Vendor delivers assets: November 8
- Final review meeting: November 10
- Campaign launch: November 15

Please adjust your calendars accordingly. Contact me with any concerns.
```

### With SCQA (For Complex Issues)
```markdown
TO: Executive Leadership
FROM: Operations Director
DATE: October 29, 2025
SUBJECT: Warehouse Expansion Decision Required

Situation: Our current warehouse operates at 95% capacity. Orders have grown 40% this year, and we're experiencing fulfillment delays.

Complication: We're turning away new business opportunities because we can't guarantee delivery times. Competitors are gaining market share. Holiday season will worsen the bottleneck.

Question: Should we expand our current facility or lease additional space?

Answer: Recommend leasing 50,000 sq ft additional space within 30 days while planning permanent expansion for next year. This provides immediate relief ($30K/month) while we design optimal long-term solution ($2M capital investment).

Detailed Analysis: [Continue with specifics if needed]

Next Steps: Approve lease authority by November 1 for December 1 occupancy.
```

### With Pyramid Principle
```markdown
TO: Engineering Team
FROM: VP Engineering
DATE: October 29, 2025
SUBJECT: New Code Review Policy Effective November 1

Answer: Starting November 1, all pull requests require two approvals before merging. This replaces our current single-approval process.

Three Key Changes:
1. Minimum approvals: Increased from 1 to 2
2. Timeline impact: PRs may take 24-48 hours longer
3. Urgent fixes: Engineering manager can override for production emergencies

Rationale: Q3 incident review identified that 60% of production bugs stemmed from single-reviewer PRs. Industry best practices and our data support two reviewers for higher quality.

Benefits:
- Catch bugs before production (projected 40% reduction)
- Knowledge sharing across team
- Better code quality and documentation
- Alignment with industry standards

Next Steps:
- GitHub settings updated: October 30
- New policy active: November 1
- Review guidelines: [Link]
- Questions: #eng-code-review Slack channel
```

## Formatting Standards

### Headers
```markdown
# Memo Title (if needed beyond subject line)
## Major Section (if longer memo)
### Subsection (rarely needed for 1-page memos)
```

### Lists

**Ordered lists** (for steps/priorities):
```markdown
1. First action item
2. Second action item
3. Third action item
```

**Unordered lists** (for features/options):
```markdown
- Option A: Description
- Option B: Description
- Option C: Description
```

### Emphasis
```markdown
**Bold:** Key terms, important concepts, action items
*Italics*: Titles, emphasis, technical terms
```

### Visual Elements (Use sparingly for 1-page constraint)
- **Tables:** For comparisons (3-5 rows max)
- **Callouts:** For critical warnings or key insights (1-2 max)
- **Bullet lists:** For enumerations (keep to 3-7 items)

## Writing Principles Integration

### Clarity (Essential)
- **Short sentences:** 15-20 words maximum
- **Simple language:** Avoid jargon for cross-functional audiences
- **Concrete examples:** "Increased 40%" not "significant increase"

✅ **Good:** "Orders grew 40% this year."
❌ **Bad:** "There has been a significant increase in order volume."

### Conciseness (Critical)
- **One page maximum** for standard memos
- **Eliminate filler:** "In order to" → "To"
- **Remove redundancy:** "Past history" → "History"

✅ **Good:** "To meet the deadline"
❌ **Bad:** "In order to meet the deadline"

### Active Voice (Preferred)
✅ **Good:** "We will launch the campaign November 15"
❌ **Bad:** "The campaign will be launched November 15"

## Common Memo Variations

### Policy Memo
- More formal tone
- Legal/compliance considerations
- Clear effective date
- Definition of terms
- Consequences of non-compliance

### Status Update Memo
- Current state vs. target
- Key metrics
- Challenges encountered
- Next milestones
- Request for input/approval

### Decision Memo
- Problem statement
- Options analyzed
- Recommendation with rationale
- Implementation plan
- Approval request

### Directive Memo
- Authoritative tone
- Clear instructions
- Compliance expectations
- Effective date
- Contact for questions

## Quality Checklist

Before sending:

- ☐ Header block complete (TO/FROM/DATE/SUBJECT)
- ☐ Subject line clearly indicates content
- ☐ Purpose stated in first paragraph (BLUF)
- ☐ Information organized logically
- ☐ Action items clearly identified with deadlines
- ☐ Tone appropriate for audience
- ☐ Grammar and spelling correct
- ☐ Length appropriate (typically 1 page)
- ☐ Formatting consistent
- ☐ Contact information available
- ☐ Reviewed against framework checklist (BLUF/Pyramid/SCQA)

## Length Guidelines

**Target:** 400-600 words (1 page)

**If exceeding 1 page:**
- Consider if this should be a brief or report instead
- Can you compress using BLUF framework?
- Can tables/bullets replace prose?
- Is all information essential?

**Compression techniques:**
- Use bullet lists instead of paragraphs
- Create comparison tables
- Remove examples (keep data only)
- Cut background (assume context)

## Tone Guidelines

**Professional but conversational:**
- Use "we" and "you" (not third person)
- Direct language
- Avoid excessive formality
- Maintain respect

✅ **Good:** "We need your approval by Friday."
❌ **Too formal:** "It is hereby requested that approval be granted by the end of business on Friday."
❌ **Too casual:** "Hey, can you approve this by Friday?"
