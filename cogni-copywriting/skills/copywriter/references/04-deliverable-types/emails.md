# Professional Emails

## Quick Reference
**Purpose:** Direct business communication
**Length:** 3-5 paragraphs maximum (200-300 words)
**Formality:** Medium (varies by relationship)
**Audience:** Internal colleagues, external stakeholders
**Best frameworks:** BLUF, SCQA
**Key principles:** Clarity, conciseness, active voice
**Critical:** 60% read on mobile—optimize for scanning

## Standard Structure

### Required Elements

1. **Subject Line** (Critical)
   - Action-oriented and specific
   - Front-load keywords
   - 6-10 words ideal
   - Indicate urgency if applicable

2. **Opening** (BLUF - First 1-2 sentences)
   - State main point immediately
   - Include action required and deadline
   - Readable without scrolling (mobile)

3. **Body** (Context - 1-3 paragraphs)
   - Essential supporting information
   - Bullet points for multiple items
   - Keep paragraphs to 3-5 sentences

4. **Closing** (Next steps)
   - Clear action items
   - Deadline reminder
   - Sign-off

## Framework Integration

### With BLUF (Most Common for Action Requests)

```markdown
**Subject:** Approve $50K Marketing Budget by Oct 30

Hi [Name],

I need your approval for the $50K Q4 marketing budget by October 30 to meet vendor deadlines.

We've negotiated favorable rates with three vendors for our holiday campaign targeting our best-performing Q3 demographic. They require commitment by month-end.

Details:
- Digital ads: $30K (15% conversion rate)
- Content production: $15K
- Analytics: $5K
- Expected revenue: $200K

Reply with approval and I'll execute contracts immediately.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### With SCQA (For Problem-Solving)

```markdown
**Subject:** Warehouse Capacity Solution Needed by Nov 1

Hi [Name],

Situation: Our warehouse is at 95% capacity and orders are up 40% this year.

Problem: We're turning away new business and competitors are gaining share. Holiday season will worsen delays.

Question: Expand current facility or lease additional space?

Recommendation: Lease 50,000 sq ft within 30 days ($30K/month) while planning permanent expansion for Q2 2026. Provides immediate relief before holiday rush.

Can we discuss approval path by Nov 1?

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

### Status Update Email

```markdown
**Subject:** Q4 Production 15% Ahead—No Action Required

Hi Team,

Q4 production is 15% ahead of schedule and 8% under budget. No action required from you.

Key metrics:
- Units: 12,500 (target: 10,850)
- Budget: $920K spent (budget: $1M)
- Quality: 1.2% defects (target: 1.5%)

Process improvements from September are working well. Expect to maintain pace through year-end.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Subject Line Best Practices

**Action-oriented and specific:**

✅ **Good Subject Lines:**
- "Approve $50K Budget by Oct 30"
- "Q4 Production 15% Ahead—No Action Required"
- "Meeting Rescheduled to Friday 2 PM"
- "Urgent: Security Breach—Immediate Action Required"

❌ **Bad Subject Lines:**
- "Budget" (vague)
- "Update" (no specificity)
- "Question" (no context)
- "Important: Please Read" (clickbait)

**Formula:** [Action/Topic] + [Key Detail] + [Deadline if applicable]

## Mobile Optimization (Critical)

**60% of emails read on phones:**

1. **Front-load critical info** in first 2 lines
2. **Keep paragraphs short** (3-5 sentences max)
3. **Use bullet points** for lists (easier to scan)
4. **Avoid wide tables** (breaks on mobile)
5. **Test subject line** (50 chars visible on mobile)

**Mobile preview test:** First 2 lines should make sense standalone.

## Writing Principles Integration

### Clarity (Essential)
- **Extremely short sentences:** 12-18 words (shorter than memos)
- **One topic per email:** Don't combine multiple requests
- **Specific actions:** "Approve by Friday" not "Please review when convenient"

### Conciseness (Critical)
- **3-5 paragraphs maximum**
- **200-300 words total**
- **Each sentence must be essential**
- **Remove pleasantries** if urgent

**Before/After Example:**

❌ **Verbose (120 words):**
```
I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out to discuss a matter that has come to my attention regarding the upcoming marketing campaign that we have been planning. As you may recall from our previous discussions, we have been working with several vendors to finalize the details...
```

✅ **Concise (40 words):**
```
I need your approval for the $50K Q4 marketing budget by October 30 to meet vendor deadlines. We've negotiated rates for our holiday campaign targeting Q3's best-performing demographic. Reply with approval and I'll execute contracts.
```

### Active Voice (Essential)
✅ **Active:** "Please approve the budget by Friday."
❌ **Passive:** "The budget needs to be approved by Friday."

## Common Email Types

### Action Request
- BLUF with specific action and deadline
- Brief rationale (why it matters)
- What happens if approved/not approved
- Easy yes/no response

### Status Update
- Lead with current status vs. target
- Key metrics or progress indicators
- Challenges if any
- Next milestones
- "No action required" if true

### Meeting Request
- Purpose and agenda upfront
- Proposed time/duration
- Required vs. optional attendees
- Prep needed
- Calendar invite attached

### Information Sharing
- Key information in first paragraph
- Why recipient needs to know
- Action if any
- Resources/links

## Tone Guidelines

### Internal Emails
- Professional but conversational
- Use first names
- Direct language
- Appropriate for relationship

### External Emails
- More formal initially
- Match recipient's tone over time
- Err on side of professionalism
- Cultural awareness

### Urgent Emails
- "Urgent:" in subject (use sparingly)
- BLUF with clear action in first sentence
- Remove all non-essential content
- Specify exact deadline

## Quality Checklist

Before sending:

- ☐ Subject line specific and action-oriented
- ☐ Main point in first 1-2 sentences
- ☐ Action required clearly stated
- ☐ Deadline included (if applicable)
- ☐ Length appropriate (200-300 words, 3-5 paragraphs)
- ☐ Tone appropriate for audience
- ☐ Grammar and spelling correct
- ☐ Mobile-friendly (first 2 lines make sense alone)
- ☐ One topic per email
- ☐ Necessary recipients only (avoid reply-all excess)

## Length Management

**Target:** 200-300 words (3-5 paragraphs)

**If exceeding 300 words:**
- Can this be a memo instead?
- Can you use bullets instead of prose?
- Can you link to details instead of including them?
- Are you covering multiple topics? (split into separate emails)

**Compression techniques:**
- Replace paragraphs with bullets
- Link to documents instead of explaining
- Remove background (assume context)
- One topic per email

## Response Optimization

Make it easy for recipient to respond:

### For Approval Requests
✅ **Good:** "Reply 'Approved' and I'll proceed."
❌ **Bad:** "Let me know your thoughts when you get a chance."

### For Meeting Requests
✅ **Good:** "Does Friday 2-3 PM work? Calendar invite attached."
❌ **Bad:** "We should find a time to discuss this soon."

### For Information Sharing
✅ **Good:** "No action required. FYI only."
❌ **Bad:** [Leaving recipient uncertain if action needed]

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Burying the Lead
❌ **Bad:** Background → Context → Request (main point on line 15)
✅ **Good:** Request → Brief rationale → Details

### Mistake 2: Multiple Topics
❌ **Bad:** Budget approval + meeting request + status update in one email
✅ **Good:** Separate emails for each topic

### Mistake 3: Vague Action
❌ **Bad:** "Please review when convenient"
✅ **Good:** "Please approve by Friday, October 30"

### Mistake 4: Novel-Length Email
❌ **Bad:** 1000 words explaining every detail
✅ **Good:** 250 words with link to full details
