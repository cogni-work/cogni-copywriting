## Proposal Writing Best Practices

**Framework Selection:**
- **FAB (Feature-Advantage-Benefit):** Product or service with distinct features - technology, consulting services with clear deliverables
- **PSB (Problem-Solution-Benefit):** Client has clear pain point - focus on problem-solving
- **Pyramid Principle:** Complex recommendation requiring structured analysis - strategic consulting

**Content Guidelines:**
- **Client-centric language:** "You will achieve" not "We will deliver"
- **Quantify everything:** Use specific metrics, timeframes, and financial impacts
- **Evidence-based:** Include case studies, testimonials, or industry benchmarks
- **Risk mitigation:** Address concerns proactively (cost, timeline, capability)
- **Clear differentiation:** Explain why your approach beats alternatives

**Structure Standards:**
- **Executive summary:** Must stand alone (some readers only read this)
- **Problem section:** Demonstrates understanding (builds trust)
- **Solution section:** Heart of proposal, requires most detail
- **Implementation:** Shows you've thought through execution
- **Investment:** Transparent pricing with ROI justification
- **Credibility:** Proves you can deliver what you promise
- **Action:** Creates urgency and clear decision path

**Length Guidelines:**
- **Simple proposal:** 3-5 pages (single service, straightforward)
- **Standard proposal:** 5-10 pages (multiple components, moderate complexity)
- **Complex proposal:** 10-20 pages (enterprise, multiple phases, detailed)
- **RFP response:** Follow RFP structure exactly, length as specified

**Tone & Style:**
- High formality (professional, polished)
- Confident but not arrogant
- Active voice ("We will deliver" not "Will be delivered")
- Future-focused and positive
- Target Flesch score: 50-60

**Visual Elements:**
- Use tables for pricing, comparisons, and ROI
- Include charts/graphs for complex data (if applicable)
- Visual element target: ~1 per page
- Bold key benefits and numbers
- Use callouts for critical points

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Focus on your company instead of client benefits
- ❌ Generic template without client-specific customization
- ❌ Vague benefits ("improve efficiency" vs "reduce processing time 35%")
- ❌ No clear differentiation from competitors
- ❌ Missing or unclear pricing
- ❌ No urgency or clear next steps
- ❌ Too technical without plain language translation
- ❌ Weak executive summary (critical!)
