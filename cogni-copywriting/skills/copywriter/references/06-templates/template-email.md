## Email Best Practices

**Subject Lines:**
- Front-load keywords (mobile shows first 30-40 characters)
- Use action verbs when requesting something
- Be specific: "Budget Approval Needed" > "Question"
- Examples:
  - "Approve Q4 Marketing Budget by Friday"
  - "Meeting Rescheduled to Nov 15 at 3pm"
  - "Project Update: Phase 2 Complete"

**Tone & Style:**
- Adjust formality to relationship and context
- Use active voice ("I recommend" not "It is recommended")
- Keep sentences under 20 words
- One idea per paragraph
- Target Flesch score: 55-65

**Length Guidelines:**
- Optimal: 200-300 words (3-5 paragraphs)
- Maximum: 400 words before considering alternative format
- If longer, consider: "See attached brief" or schedule a meeting

**Mobile Optimization:**
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Scannable structure with clear breaks
- Key info in first 2 sentences
- Bullets for lists (easier to scan than prose)

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- ❌ Burying the main point in paragraph 3
- ❌ No clear call to action
- ❌ Vague subject line ("Following up")
- ❌ Overly long paragraphs (mobile readers will skip)
- ❌ Missing deadline or urgency indicator
- ❌ Too formal or too casual for relationship

**Framework Selection:**
- **Use BLUF when:** Action needed, busy recipient, time-sensitive
- **Use SCQA when:** Building case, persuading, complex situation
- **Use Inverted Pyramid when:** Sharing news or updates
