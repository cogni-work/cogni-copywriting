# PSB Framework (Problem-Solution-Benefit)

## Quick Reference
**Best for:** One-pagers, proposals, marketing content, sales collateral
**Structure:** Problem → Solution → Benefit
**When to use:** Marketing, sales, customer-facing, pain-point driven
**Formality:** Medium
**Key principle:** Lead with customer pain, show how you solve it, emphasize value

## Core Structure

PSB is a marketing framework that connects customer pain points to your solution and emphasizes tangible benefits.

### Three Components

1. **Problem**: Customer's pain point, challenge, or frustration (what keeps them up at night)
2. **Solution**: Your product/service/approach (how you address the problem)
3. **Benefit**: Tangible value delivered (what they gain, often quantified)

### Persuasion Flow
```
PROBLEM (Pain) → SOLUTION (Your offering) → BENEFIT (Value gained)
```

**Psychological Effect:** Acknowledges pain (empathy) → Offers relief (solution) → Shows value (motivation to act)

## Application Pattern

### Basic Template
```markdown
## [Product/Service Name]

**Problem**: {Customer pain point with specifics and impact}

**Solution**: {Your offering and how it works}

**Benefit**: {Quantified value and outcomes}
```

### Extended Format (One-Pager)
```markdown
# [Compelling Headline Addressing Pain]

## The Challenge
{2-3 sentences describing customer problem with emotional resonance}

## Our Solution
{2-3 paragraphs explaining your approach}
- Key feature 1
- Key feature 2
- Key feature 3

## Your Benefits
{Bullet list of concrete outcomes}
- Benefit 1: [quantified impact]
- Benefit 2: [quantified impact]
- Benefit 3: [quantified impact]

## Next Steps
[Clear call to action]
```

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: SaaS Product One-Pager

**Problem**: Marketing teams waste 15-20 hours weekly manually compiling reports from disparate analytics tools. Data inconsistencies lead to poor decisions and missed opportunities.

**Solution**: AnalyticsCo consolidates data from 50+ marketing platforms into unified dashboards with automated reporting. Set up in 30 minutes, no coding required.

**Benefit**: Reclaim 20 hours/week ($40K annual productivity gain), make data-driven decisions 5x faster, increase campaign ROI by 25% through better insights.

### Example 2: Consulting Service Proposal

**Problem**: You're losing European market opportunities to local competitors. Three major prospects chose European vendors last quarter, representing $5M in lost annual revenue. Local presence and support are deal-breakers.

**Solution**: Acquire CompanyX for $50M. Instant European presence with established operations, complementary product lines, and shared customer base. Turnkey market entry without 3-year build-out.

**Benefit**: Capture $5M annual revenue immediately, payback in 3 years with 25% IRR, establish market leadership before competitors expand. Avoid $8M+ infrastructure investment and 3-year delay.

### Example 3: Service Offering

**Problem**: Your code review process creates 3-5 day release delays. Reviews pile up, engineers context-switch constantly, quality suffers despite time investment. Teams frustrated, customers waiting.

**Solution**: CodeReview Pro automates routine checks, intelligently assigns reviewers based on expertise, enforces 24-hour SLAs with escalation. Integrates with GitHub/GitLab in 10 minutes.

**Benefit**: Cut review time by 75% (72→18 hours), double release frequency, maintain quality (1.2% defect rate), improve team morale. ROI in first month.

## Deliverable-Specific Guidance

### For One-Pagers
- Problem: 2-3 sentences, emotional + quantified impact
- Solution: 3-5 bullet points of key features
- Benefit: 3-5 bullet points with metrics
- Add: Visual elements (icons, charts), testimonial, CTA

### For Proposals
- Problem: 1-2 paragraphs showing you understand their pain
- Solution: 2-3 pages detailing your approach
- Benefit: ROI calculation, before/after comparison, case studies
- Add: Pricing, timeline, next steps

### For Marketing Content
- Problem: Headline + 1 paragraph (emotional hook)
- Solution: 2-3 paragraphs with feature highlights
- Benefit: Bullet list + customer testimonials
- Add: Social proof, demo CTA

### For Sales Collateral
- Problem: Customer quote expressing frustration
- Solution: "How We Help" section with clear explanation
- Benefit: Case study with specific metrics
- Add: Comparison table, pricing options

## Integration with Writing Principles

**Clarity:** Each component must be immediately understandable
**Concrete language:** Specific pain points and quantified benefits
**Customer focus:** "You" language, not "We" focus
**Active voice:** "You'll reduce costs" not "Costs will be reduced"

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Generic Problem
❌ **Bad:** "Businesses face challenges with efficiency."
✅ **Good:** "You waste 15 hours/week manually compiling reports, costing $40K annually."

### Mistake 2: Feature-Focused Solution (Not Benefit)
❌ **Bad:** "Our platform has advanced AI algorithms."
✅ **Good:** "AI automation eliminates 15 hours of manual work weekly."

### Mistake 3: Vague Benefits
❌ **Bad:** "You'll see significant improvements."
✅ **Good:** "Reduce costs by 30% ($2M savings) and improve speed by 75%."

### Mistake 4: Disconnected Problem-Solution
❌ **Bad:** Problem about cost, solution about features unrelated to cost
✅ **Good:** Clear line from cost problem → cost-reducing solution → quantified savings benefit

## Problem Identification Best Practices

Effective problems are:

- **Specific:** Not "inefficiency" but "15 hours/week on manual reports"
- **Quantified:** Include time, money, or opportunity cost
- **Emotional:** Connect to frustration or aspiration
- **Urgent:** Show why solving this matters now
- **Relatable:** Customer sees themselves in the description

**Formula:** "[Specific pain point] costs you [quantified impact], causing [negative consequence]."

## Benefit Quantification

Always quantify benefits when possible:

✅ **Strong Benefits:**

- "Save 20 hours/week ($40K annual productivity gain)"
- "Reduce inventory costs by 30% ($2M savings)"
- "Increase conversion rates from 8% to 23% (+188%)"
- "Cut time-to-market by 50% (6 months → 3 months)"

❌ **Weak Benefits:**

- "Save time and money"
- "Improve efficiency"
- "Better results"
- "Increased productivity"

**Formula:** [Action verb] [metric] by [amount] ([dollar/time value])

## PSB Checklist

Before finalizing:

- ☐ Problem is specific and quantified
- ☐ Problem resonates emotionally
- ☐ Solution directly addresses stated problem
- ☐ Solution explanation is clear (not jargon-heavy)
- ☐ Benefits are quantified with metrics
- ☐ Clear line from Problem → Solution → Benefit
- ☐ Customer-focused ("you" language)
- ☐ Call to action included

## PSB vs. FAB

| Framework | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| PSB | Customer pain points | Sales, proposals, pain-driven marketing |
| FAB | Product features | Product launches, technical audiences, feature-rich offerings |

**When to choose:**

- **PSB:** When customer knows they have a problem
- **FAB:** When customer needs education about capabilities

**Hybrid:** Use PSB structure but incorporate FAB details in Solution section.
