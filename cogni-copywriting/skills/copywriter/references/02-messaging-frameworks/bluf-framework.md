# BLUF Framework (Bottom Line Up Front)

## Quick Reference
**Best for:** Emails, memos, briefs, executive summaries
**Structure:** Main point → Context → Details
**When to use:** Time-sensitive communication, busy executives, action needed
**Formality:** Medium-high (military/government origin)
**Key principle:** State conclusion in first 1-2 sentences, no buildup

## Core Structure

BLUF is a military communication framework that demands the main point appear immediately—no context, no preamble, just the bottom line.

### Three-Layer Structure

1. **BLUF Statement** (1-2 sentences)
   - State the main point, key information, or required action immediately
   - Include: Who, What, When, Where, Why (5 Ws)
   - Add timeline/urgency if applicable

2. **Context/Background** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Provide essential supporting information that explains the BLUF
   - Only include what's necessary to understand the situation

3. **Details** (2-4 paragraphs or bullets)
   - Additional relevant facts, considerations, or evidence
   - Supporting data, implications, next steps

## Application Pattern

### Basic Template

```markdown
# [Subject Line: Action verb + key outcome]

BLUF: [Main point or required action with deadline]

Background: [Why this matters, essential context]

Details:
- [Supporting fact 1]
- [Supporting fact 2]
- [Supporting fact 3]

Next Steps: [Clear action items]
```

### Ultra-Compressed (Email)

```markdown
Subject: Approve Marketing Budget by Oct 30

BLUF: I need your approval for $50K Q4 marketing budget by October 30 to meet vendor deadlines.

Context: Negotiated favorable rates with three vendors for holiday campaign targeting our best-performing Q3 demographic.

Next Steps: Reply with approval, I'll execute contracts immediately.
```

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Action Request Email

**Subject:** Approve Marketing Budget by Oct 30

**BLUF:** I need your approval for the $50K Q4 marketing campaign budget by October 30 to meet vendor deadlines.

**Context:** We've negotiated favorable rates with three vendors for our holiday campaign, but they require commitment by month-end. The campaign targets our best-performing demographic from Q3.

**Details:**

- Digital ads: $30K (proven 15% conversion rate)
- Content production: $15K (video + blog series)
- Analytics tools: $5K (ROI tracking)
- Expected revenue: $200K based on Q3 performance

**Next Steps:** Reply with approval, and I'll execute vendor contracts immediately.

### Example 2: Status Update Memo

**TO:** Executive Team
**FROM:** Operations Director
**DATE:** October 29, 2025
**SUBJECT:** Q4 Production Ahead of Schedule

**BLUF:** Q4 production is 15% ahead of schedule and 8% under budget. No action required.

**Context:** Process improvements implemented in September are driving efficiency gains. Team morale high, quality metrics stable.

**Key Metrics:**

- Units produced: 12,500 (target: 10,850)
- Budget spent: $920K (budget: $1M)
- Defect rate: 1.2% (target: 1.5%)
- On-time delivery: 98% (target: 95%)

**Outlook:** Expect to maintain pace through year-end.

### Example 3: Decision Brief

**SUBJECT:** Warehouse Expansion Decision Required

**BLUF:** Recommend leasing 50,000 sq ft additional warehouse space within 30 days at $30K/month while planning permanent expansion for Q2 2026.

**Context:** Current facility at 95% capacity, orders up 40% this year, turning away new business. Immediate relief needed before holiday season.

**Analysis:**

| Option | Cost | Timeline | Risk |
|--------|------|----------|------|
| Lease space | $30K/mo | 30 days | Low |
| Build new | $2M capital | 18 months | Medium |
| Do nothing | $5M lost revenue | N/A | High |

**Recommendation:** Lease provides immediate relief while we design optimal long-term solution.

**Next Steps:** Approve lease authority by Nov 1 for Dec 1 occupancy.

## Deliverable-Specific Guidance

### For Memos
- Place BLUF immediately after header (TO/FROM/DATE/SUBJECT)
- Keep BLUF to single paragraph
- Use subject line to preview BLUF content
- Total length: 1 page maximum

### For Emails
- Subject line = condensed BLUF
- First 2 lines = full BLUF (before any greeting)
- Mobile-optimize (60% read on phones)
- Total length: 3-5 paragraphs

### For Briefs
- BLUF becomes executive summary
- Follow with situation analysis
- End with recommendations
- Total length: 1-3 pages

### For Executive Summaries
- BLUF approach throughout entire summary
- Each section starts with conclusion
- Details follow for those who need them
- Total length: 1-2 pages maximum

## When NOT to Use BLUF

### Inappropriate Contexts

- **Delivering bad news requiring diplomacy:** Build context first to soften blow
- **Persuading skeptical audiences:** Need to build case before conclusion
- **Creative storytelling:** Suspense adds value
- **Cultural contexts:** Some cultures prefer context before conclusions
- **Teaching complex concepts:** Learning requires scaffolding
- **First-time communication:** Relationship building needs context

**Rule:** Use BLUF when recipient values speed over narrative. Use SCQA when building case matters.

## Integration with Writing Principles

**Clarity:** BLUF structure enforces clarity by forcing main point upfront
**Conciseness:** Pressure to state bottom line first eliminates verbosity
**Active voice:** "You need to approve..." not "Approval is needed..."
**Concrete language:** Specific actions, deadlines, numbers in BLUF

## BLUF Formula

### The 5 Ws in BLUF

- **Who:** Who needs to act / who is affected?
- **What:** What is the action / decision / information?
- **When:** When is the deadline / when did it happen?
- **Where:** Where does this apply? (if relevant)
- **Why:** Why does this matter / why now?

**Example:** "Operations [WHO] must approve [WHAT] the warehouse lease [WHAT] by November 1 [WHEN] to secure December occupancy [WHY]."

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Fake BLUF (Still Burying the Lead)
❌ **Bad:** "I'm writing to discuss the marketing budget situation and provide an update on our Q4 planning efforts."
✅ **Good:** "I need your approval for the $50K Q4 marketing budget by October 30."

### Mistake 2: Missing Urgency/Deadline
❌ **Bad:** "We should consider approving the budget soon."
✅ **Good:** "Approve the $50K budget by October 30 to meet vendor deadlines."

### Mistake 3: Too Much Context Before BLUF
❌ **Bad:** [2 paragraphs of background] → [BLUF buried in paragraph 3]
✅ **Good:** [BLUF first sentence] → [Background follows]

### Mistake 4: Vague BLUF
❌ **Bad:** "We need to discuss some budget issues."
✅ **Good:** "Approve $50K Q4 marketing budget by Oct 30."

## Subject Line Best Practices

Subject lines for BLUF communications must be action-oriented and specific:

✅ **Good Subject Lines:**

- "Approve $50K Budget by Oct 30"
- "Q4 Production 15% Ahead—No Action Required"
- "Lease Warehouse Space by Nov 1"
- "Security Breach Detected—Immediate Action Required"

❌ **Bad Subject Lines:**

- "Budget Update" (vague)
- "Q4 Status" (no action/outcome)
- "Warehouse Situation" (no direction)
- "Important: Please Read" (clickbait)

## BLUF Checklist

Before sending:

- ☐ Main point in first 1-2 sentences
- ☐ Action required clearly stated
- ☐ Deadline included (if applicable)
- ☐ Subject line previews BLUF
- ☐ 5 Ws answered in BLUF
- ☐ Could busy executive understand without reading further?
- ☐ Urgency/importance clear
- ☐ No preamble or throat-clearing

## BLUF vs. Pyramid vs. SCQA

| Framework | Best For | Opens With |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| BLUF | Time-sensitive, action requests | Required action + deadline |
| Pyramid | Strategic recommendations | Conclusion + MECE arguments |
| SCQA | Problem-solving narratives | Context → Problem → Solution |

**Hybrid:** Can use BLUF for email + Pyramid for attached analysis + SCQA for proposal narrative.
