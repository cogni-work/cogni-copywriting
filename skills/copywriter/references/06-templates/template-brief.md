## Writing Guidelines for Briefs

**Structure Tips:**
- **Pyramid Principle:** Start with answer, then provide supporting arguments
- **MECE Arguments:** Make sections Mutually Exclusive and Collectively Exhaustive
- **Parallel Structure:** Keep headers at same level grammatically parallel

**Content Guidelines:**
- Lead with quantified impact when possible
- Use tables for comparisons and structured data
- Include visual elements every 2-3 paragraphs
- Cite sources for external data or research
- Target Flesch score: 50-60 (standard difficulty)

**Length Targets:**
- **1-page brief:** 400-600 words, single major recommendation
- **2-page brief:** 700-1000 words, 2-3 supporting arguments
- **3-page brief:** 1000-1500 words, complex analysis with multiple options

**Formatting Standards:**
- Maximum 3 header levels (# ## ###)
- Paragraphs: 3-5 sentences average
- Use horizontal rules (---) to separate major sections
- Bold key terms and critical numbers
- Use callouts or blockquotes for key insights

**Tone & Formality:**
- High formality (executive audience)
- Confident, assertive language
- Active voice (80%+ target)
- Avoid hedging ("clearly shows" not "seems to suggest")
- Professional but not stuffy

**Common Pitfalls:**
- ❌ Burying recommendation in middle of document
- ❌ No clear decision request or timeline
- ❌ Arguments that overlap or have gaps (not MECE)
- ❌ Too much data without interpretation
- ❌ Missing financial impact when relevant
- ❌ Unclear next steps or ownership

**Framework Selection:**
- **BLUF:** Decision needed quickly, clear recommendation
- **Pyramid:** Complex recommendation needing structured support (McKinsey-style)
- **SCQA:** Building case for change, need to establish problem urgency
