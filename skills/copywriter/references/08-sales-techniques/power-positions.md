# Power Positions Enhancement Guide

Reference for enhancing Power Positions content while preserving IS-DOES-MEANS structure.

Based on "Conversations That Win the Complex Sale" by Erik Peterson & Tim Riesterer (Corporate Visions).

---

## When to Use

Load this reference when:
- `MODE: sales` is specified
- Content contains Power Positions (IS-DOES-MEANS blocks)
- Document type is sales proposal or value story

---

## IS-DOES-MEANS Structure

Power Positions follow a three-layer message pyramid:

| Layer | Purpose | Enhancement Focus |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **IS** | What the capability IS (features/functions) | Specificity, concreteness |
| **DOES** | What customer can DO better | Quantification, time/value metrics |
| **MEANS** | What it MEANS to them (outcome) | Emotional resonance, strategic impact |

**Critical Rule:** Never merge layers. Each serves a distinct purpose in the message hierarchy.

---

## Structure Markers (PRESERVE EXACTLY)

These markers MUST remain unchanged:

```markdown
**IS**:
**DOES**:
**MEANS**:
**Proof**:
**Addresses**:
**Competitor Gap**:
```

Also preserve:
- Power Position numbering (`### Power Position #N:`)
- Need references (`unconsidered need #N`)
- Citation markers (`[P3-N]`)

## Enhancement Rules by Layer

### IS Layer (Capability)

**Goal:** Make the capability specific and concrete.

| Weak | Enhanced |
|------|----------|
| "AI-powered platform" | "AI-powered risk scoring engine trained on 10 years of industry data" |
| "Cloud solution" | "ISO 27001-certified private cloud with <10ms latency" |
| "Modern technology" | "Real-time streaming architecture processing 1M events/second" |

**Techniques:**
- Add specifics: data volume, timeframes, certifications
- Remove generic buzzwords: "best-in-class", "industry-leading"
- Include technical differentiators that support uniqueness

---

### DOES Layer (Power Statement)

**Goal:** Quantify the action and outcome the customer can achieve.

| Weak | Enhanced |
|------|----------|
| "Helps improve operations" | "Identify supply chain disruptions 3 weeks before impact" |
| "Makes decisions faster" | "Reduce decision cycle from 2 weeks to 48 hours" |
| "Increases efficiency" | "Automate 80% of manual data reconciliation" |

**Techniques:**
- Apply Number Plays: percentages, timeframes, multipliers
- Use action verbs: Identify, Reduce, Automate, Eliminate, Accelerate
- Include before/after contrast when possible
- Quantify the delta: "from X to Y" or "by N%"

---

### MEANS Layer (Outcome)

**Goal:** Strengthen emotional and strategic resonance for executives.

| Weak | Enhanced |
|------|----------|
| "Better business outcomes" | "Never be caught off-guard by supplier failures again" |
| "Improved performance" | "Protect revenue and customer commitments quarter after quarter" |
| "Cost savings" | "Redirect $2M annually from firefighting to strategic initiatives" |

**Techniques:**
- Apply Power Words: protect, transform, eliminate, breakthrough, exclusive
- Connect to executive concerns: revenue, risk, reputation, growth
- Use emotional triggers: peace of mind, confidence, control
- Frame as transformation, not incremental improvement

## Enhancement Examples

### Before Enhancement

```markdown
### Power Position #1: Advanced Analytics

**IS**: Analytics platform with AI

**DOES**: Helps improve decision-making

**MEANS**: Better outcomes for the business

**Proof**: We work with Fortune 500 companies
**Addresses**: Operational efficiency
**Competitor Gap**: We have more experience
```

### After Enhancement

```markdown
### Power Position #1: Predictive Risk Intelligence

**IS**: AI-powered risk scoring engine trained on 10 years of manufacturing industry data, processing 50K+ supplier signals daily

**DOES**: Identify supply chain disruptions 3 weeks before they impact operations, reducing emergency response costs by 60%

**MEANS**: Never be caught off-guard by supplier failures again - protect revenue and customer commitments with confidence

**Proof**: Prevented $4.2M in losses for similar manufacturer in 2024; 94% prediction accuracy across 200+ incidents
**Addresses**: Hidden supply chain vulnerabilities (unconsidered need #2)
**Competitor Gap**: Generic risk tools use public data; proprietary industry signals provide 3-week advance warning vs. 3-day industry average
```

## Quality Validation

After enhancement, each Power Position must pass:

| Criterion | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| IS specificity | Contains concrete details (numbers, names, specs)? |
| DOES quantification | Includes measurable outcome (%, time, amount)? |
| MEANS resonance | Would an executive care about this outcome? |
| Proof credibility | Evidence is specific and verifiable? |
| Structure preserved | All markers present and correctly formatted? |

---

## Integration with Impact Techniques

When MODE: sales, combine Power Positions enhancement with standard impact techniques:

| Technique | Application to Power Positions |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Number Plays | Primary focus on DOES layer |
| Power Words | Primary focus on MEANS layer |
| Rule of Three | Use when listing multiple capabilities in IS |
| Comparative Anchoring | Use in Competitor Gap field |

---

## Anti-Patterns (AVOID)

1. **Layer Bleeding:** Putting outcomes in IS or features in MEANS
2. **Generic Proof:** "Fortune 500 clients" without specific results
3. **Unquantified DOES:** Action without measurable impact
4. **Bland MEANS:** Business jargon instead of executive resonance
5. **Merged Structure:** Combining IS-DOES or DOES-MEANS into single statement
