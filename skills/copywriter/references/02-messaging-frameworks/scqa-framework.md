# SCQA Framework (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer)

## Quick Reference
**Best for:** Memos, briefs, proposals, reports
**Structure:** Situation → Complication → Question → Answer
**When to use:** Narrative flow needed, problem-solving focus, building urgency
**Formality:** Medium-high
**Key principle:** Build tension before revealing solution

## Core Structure

The SCQA framework creates a narrative arc that engages readers by establishing context, introducing tension, and then resolving with your answer.

### Four Components

1. **Situation**: Current state or context (what exists today)
2. **Complication**: Problem or challenge (why it matters, what's at stake)
3. **Question**: The key question that must be answered (often implicit)
4. **Answer**: Your solution or recommendation (the payoff)

### Narrative Flow

```
SITUATION (Context)
    ↓
COMPLICATION (Problem)
    ↓
QUESTION (What to do?)
    ↓
ANSWER (Your solution)
```

**Psychological Effect:** Builds tension (S-C-Q) then releases it (A), making the answer more impactful.

## Application Pattern

### Basic Template

```markdown
## Executive Summary

**Situation**: {Describe current landscape or context}

**Challenge**: {Identify the problem or gap}

**Question**: {State the key question being addressed}

**Solution**: {Present your answer or recommendation}
```

### Extended Format

```markdown
## Situation
{2-3 paragraphs: Set the scene, provide context}

## Complication
{2-3 paragraphs: Introduce the problem, show impact, create urgency}

## Question
{1 paragraph: Frame the central question explicitly or implicitly}

## Answer
{3-5 paragraphs: Present solution, supporting arguments, evidence}
```

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Business Memo

**Situation**: "Deep research projects require systematic methodology to ensure quality and reproducibility."

**Complication**: "Current tools lack structured frameworks, leading to inconsistent results and lost context. Teams waste 40% of time rediscovering information."

**Question**: "How can we systematize deep research to improve quality and efficiency?"

**Answer**: "A 13-phase pipeline with entity-based architecture solves this through automated tracking and validation, reducing rework by 60%."

### Example 2: Strategic Proposal

**Situation:**
Our company serves the North American market with $50M annual revenue. Market research shows European demand for our product category growing at 15% annually.

**Complication:**
We're losing European opportunities to local competitors. Three major prospects chose European vendors last quarter citing local presence and support. This represents $5M in lost revenue annually.

**Question:**
How do we capture European market share without building operations from scratch?

**Answer:**
Acquire CompanyX for $50M. They have established European presence, complementary product lines, and shared customer base. Payback in 3 years with 25% IRR.

### Example 3: Executive Briefing

**Situation:**
Our warehouse operates at 95% capacity. Orders have grown 40% this year.

**Complication:**
We're experiencing fulfillment delays and turning away new business. Competitors are gaining market share. Holiday season will worsen the bottleneck.

**Question:**
Should we expand our current facility or lease additional space?

**Answer:**
Lease 50,000 sq ft additional space within 30 days ($30K/month) while planning permanent expansion for next year ($2M capital investment). Provides immediate relief while we design optimal long-term solution.

## Deliverable-Specific Guidance

### For Memos
- Keep each component to 1-2 paragraphs
- Question can be implicit (in subject line)
- Answer should include action items
- Total length: 1 page

### For Briefs
- Compress to 3-5 sentences per component
- Use bold labels (Situation:, Challenge:, Solution:)
- Bullet points for supporting details
- Total length: 1-3 pages

### For Proposals
- Situation = market context
- Complication = client pain points
- Question = how to solve their problem
- Answer = your solution with evidence
- Follow with detailed implementation plan

### For Reports
- Extended format works well
- Situation = literature review or background
- Complication = research gap or problem
- Question = research question
- Answer = findings and recommendations

## When to Use vs. Pyramid Principle

### Use SCQA When:
- Building case for skeptical audience
- Complex problem needs context
- Emotional engagement valuable
- Narrative flow aids understanding

### Use Pyramid When:
- Time-sensitive communication
- Executive wants answer immediately
- Recommendation is straightforward
- Consulting/McKinsey-style expected

**Hybrid Approach:** SCQA in executive summary, Pyramid in body sections.

## Integration with Writing Principles

**Clarity:** Each component must be crystal clear
**Conciseness:** Keep S-C-Q brief to reach Answer quickly
**Active voice:** Essential in Answer section to show confidence
**Concrete language:** Especially in Complication (specific impacts)

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Too Much Situation
❌ **Bad:** 5 paragraphs of background before getting to problem
✅ **Good:** 2-3 sentences of essential context, move to complication

### Mistake 2: Weak Complication
❌ **Bad:** "This might cause some issues."
✅ **Good:** "This costs $5M annually and risks market share loss."

### Mistake 3: No Tension
❌ **Bad:** Situation is fine, no real problem
✅ **Good:** Clear gap between current state (S) and needs (C)

### Mistake 4: Question Not Obvious
❌ **Bad:** Complication doesn't naturally lead to question
✅ **Good:** Question is the logical "so what do we do?" after complication

## Compression Techniques

For tight page constraints:

### Ultra-Compressed (Email/Brief)
```markdown
**Context**: {1 sentence}
**Problem**: {1 sentence}
**Solution**: {2-3 sentences}
```

### Medium (Memo)
```markdown
**Situation**: {1 paragraph, 3-5 sentences}
**Challenge**: {1 paragraph, 3-5 sentences}
**Recommendation**: {2 paragraphs with details}
```

### Full (Report)
```markdown
## Background (Situation)
{Multiple paragraphs with evidence}

## Problem Statement (Complication)
{Multiple paragraphs showing impact}

## Research Question
{Explicit question statement}

## Findings & Recommendations (Answer)
{Extensive detail with supporting evidence}
```

## SCQA Checklist

Before finalizing:

- ☐ Situation provides essential context (not excessive background)
- ☐ Complication shows clear problem with impact
- ☐ Tension builds naturally from S to C
- ☐ Question is obvious (explicit or implicit)
- ☐ Answer directly addresses the question
- ☐ Solution is compelling and actionable
- ☐ Appropriate length for deliverable type
- ☐ Could reader follow the logic flow?

## Variations

### SCR (Situation-Complication-Resolution)
Omit explicit Question, go straight from Complication to Resolution. Works when question is obvious.

### SCQAI (Add Implications)
Add "Implications" section after Answer to show broader impact.

### Multiple Complications
For complex problems, present 2-3 complications before the question.
