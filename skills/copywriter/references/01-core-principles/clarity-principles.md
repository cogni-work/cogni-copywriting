# Clarity Principles (Wolf-Schneider)

## Quick Reference
**Use when:** Every deliverable type
**Core principle:** Write so everyone can understand
**Key techniques:** Short sentences, concrete language, simple words
**Target:** 15-20 words per sentence in business writing

## Detailed Principles

### 1. Use Short Sentences
Long sentences confuse and tire readers. Aim for 15-20 words per sentence in business writing.

**Rule:** Main things belong in main clauses. Don't bury your point in subordinate clauses.

❌ **Avoid:** "Due to difficult weather conditions caused by an unexpected cold snap during the night, rescue workers were unable to reach the stranded hikers in time."

✅ **Better:** "An unexpected cold snap prevented rescue workers from reaching the hikers in time."

**Metrics:**
- Target: 15-20 words per sentence average
- Maximum: 25 words for complex ideas
- Ideal: Vary between 10-25 words for rhythm

### 2. Choose Concrete Language
Avoid abstract terms. Use concrete, vivid language that creates mental images.

❌ **Avoid:** "The company experienced a significant increase in profits."
✅ **Better:** "The company doubled its profits."

❌ **Avoid:** "We need to implement an optimization strategy for our operational processes."
✅ **Better:** "We need to streamline our workflows."

**Pattern:**
- Abstract → Concrete: "significant increase" → "doubled", "tripled", "up 40%"
- Vague → Specific: "soon" → "next Tuesday", "many" → "127", "large" → "500 employees"
- General → Particular: "items" → "laptops", "issues" → "login failures"

### 3. Write Simply
Avoid technical jargon and complicated formulations. Aim for universal comprehension.

❌ **Avoid:** "The implementation of the new software solution led to an optimization of operational processes."
✅ **Better:** "The new software improves company workflows."

❌ **Avoid:** "We are experiencing suboptimal performance metrics in the customer engagement vertical."
✅ **Better:** "Customer engagement numbers are down."

**Test:** Could a smart 14-year-old understand this? If not, simplify.

## Application by Deliverable Type

**Reports:** Apply to body sections; maintain professional tone while ensuring clarity.
**Memos:** Essential for busy internal audiences; extremely short sentences preferred (12-18 words).
**Emails:** Critical—readers scan quickly; every word must earn its place.
**Proposals:** Balance technical precision with accessibility for mixed audiences.
**Briefs:** Extreme clarity required; executives read in 2-3 minutes.
**One-Pagers:** Every sentence must be crystal clear; no room for confusion.

## Integration with Frameworks

- **BLUF:** Clarity ensures the bottom line is immediately understood
- **Pyramid Principle:** Clear language makes the logical structure obvious
- **SCQA:** Simple language prevents confusion in problem/solution narrative
- **STAR:** Concrete examples demonstrate clear cause-and-effect
- **FAB:** Features must be clearly stated before advantages make sense

## Common Clarity Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Buried Main Point
❌ **Bad:** "In light of recent market developments and considering various stakeholder inputs, it has been determined that a restructuring initiative would be beneficial."
✅ **Good:** "We're restructuring the division. Market changes and stakeholder feedback support this decision."

### Mistake 2: Abstract Language Chains
❌ **Bad:** "Utilization of synergistic implementation methodologies"
✅ **Good:** "Use combined methods"

### Mistake 3: Passive Voice Obscuring Actor
❌ **Bad:** "The decision was made to proceed."
✅ **Good:** "The executive team decided to proceed."

## Clarity Checklist

Before finalizing content, verify:

- ☐ Average sentence length 15-20 words
- ☐ No sentences exceed 30 words
- ☐ Concrete words replace abstract terms (80%+ concrete)
- ☐ Technical jargon explained or eliminated
- ☐ Main point in main clause (not buried in dependent clauses)
- ☐ Active voice used (not passive)
- ☐ Specific numbers replace vague quantifiers
- ☐ Could a non-specialist understand this?

## Wolf-Schneider Core Rules (Summary)

1. **Short sentences win**: 15-20 words average
2. **Main things in main clauses**: Don't bury your point
3. **Concrete beats abstract**: "Doubled" not "significant increase"
4. **Simple beats complex**: Say it plainly first
5. **Specific beats general**: "127 customers" not "many customers"
