# Arc Technique Map

When polishing a narrative that has YAML frontmatter containing `arc_id`, load this reference to apply element-specific polish techniques. The copywriter does NOT create or restructure arcs — it strengthens the narrative techniques already present within each element.

## Arc Detection

Check document frontmatter for `arc_id` field:

```yaml
---
arc_id: corporate-visions  # Triggers arc-aware polish
---
```

If `arc_id` is present, activate arc-aware polish mode and load this map.

## Technique Map by Arc

### corporate-visions

| Element | Heading Text | Techniques to Strengthen | Number Play Variant | Word Target |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Hook | (opening paragraphs) | Pyramid Principle (answer-first) | Hero number isolation | 150-200 |
| Why Change | Why Change | PSB, Contrast Structure | Ratio framing, specific quantification | 400-500 |
| Why Now | Why Now | Forcing Functions (2-3 stacked), timelines | Before/after contrast | 300-400 |
| Why You | Why You | IS-DOES-MEANS, You-Phrasing | Comparative anchoring | 400-500 |
| Why Pay | Why Pay | Compound Impact Calculation | Compound impact (3-4 cost dimensions, 3-year horizon) | 200-300 |

**Element-specific polish rules:**

- **Why Change:** Verify PSB pattern intact (Problem → Solution → Benefit). Strengthen contrast structures ("Most think X. Research shows Y."). Enhance evidence-based reframing language. Do NOT flatten into generic problem statement.
- **Why Now:** Verify 2-3 forcing functions are stacked with specific timelines. Replace vague urgency ("soon", "quickly") with dates and deadlines. Strengthen before/after contrasts for early vs. late movers.
- **Why You:** Verify IS-DOES-MEANS layers are distinct. Apply You-Phrasing in DOES layer ("You reduce..." not "Organizations can reduce..."). Enhance Number Plays in DOES layer. Do NOT merge layers.
- **Why Pay:** Verify compound impact calculation stacks 3-4 cost dimensions. Ensure 3-year horizon is explicit. Strengthen closing ratio comparison. Keep this element concise — it's the punchline.

### technology-futures

| Element | Heading Text | Techniques to Strengthen | Number Play Variant | Word Target |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Hook | (opening paragraphs) | Pyramid Principle | Hero number isolation | 150-200 |
| What's Emerging | What's Emerging | Maturity signals, capability quantification | Specific quantification, before/after | 350-450 |
| What's Converging | What's Converging | Multiplicative framing (not additive) | Compound impact | 350-450 |
| What's Possible | What's Possible | Concrete scenarios, opportunity windows | Comparative anchoring, ratio framing | 350-450 |
| What's Required | What's Required | Prerequisites, sequencing logic | Before/after contrast | 200-350 |

**Element-specific polish rules:**

- **What's Emerging:** Strengthen maturity signals (lab → pilot → production). Replace hype language with evidence from early adopters. Quantify capability improvements with specific numbers.
- **What's Converging:** Ensure multiplicative framing ("together they enable X, which neither achieves alone"). Do NOT reduce to a list of technologies. Strengthen timing explanation (why converging now).
- **What's Possible:** Make scenarios concrete (specific use cases, not abstract opportunities). Add quantified value potential and opportunity window timelines. Connect to competitive positioning.
- **What's Required:** Strengthen specificity of prerequisites. Ensure build timelines and sequencing logic are explicit. Clarify make/buy/partner decisions.

### competitive-intelligence

| Element | Heading Text | Techniques to Strengthen | Number Play Variant | Word Target |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Hook | (opening paragraphs) | Pyramid Principle | Hero number isolation | 150-200 |
| Landscape | Landscape | PSB, segment decomposition | Specific quantification (market share, revenue) | 350-450 |
| Shifts | Shifts | Contrast Structure, momentum analysis | Before/after contrast, ratio framing | 300-400 |
| Positioning | Positioning | IS-DOES-MEANS for gap identification | Comparative anchoring | 400-500 |
| Implications | Implications | Time-bound actions, competitive response | Before/after contrast | 250-350 |

**Element-specific polish rules:**

- **Landscape:** Strengthen market structure quantification (share %, revenue, capability scores). Ensure competitive bases are mapped (cost/differentiation/focus). Do NOT turn into narrative — this is analytical.
- **Shifts:** Strengthen contrast between static positions and dynamic momentum. Quantify velocity indicators (share trends, investment rates). Ensure strategic moves have timelines.
- **Positioning:** Verify uncontested spaces are clearly identified. Strengthen capability gap specificity. Ensure timing advantages are quantified.
- **Implications:** Verify three time horizons present (0-6mo, 6-18mo, 18-36mo). Ensure competitive response scenarios are considered. Make actions specific and measurable.

### strategic-foresight

| Element | Heading Text | Techniques to Strengthen | Number Play Variant | Word Target |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Hook | (opening paragraphs) | Pyramid Principle | Hero number isolation | 150-200 |
| Signals | Signals | Weak signal identification, forcing functions | Specific quantification | 300-400 |
| Scenarios | Scenarios | Contrast Structure (2-3 distinct futures) | Comparative anchoring | 400-500 |
| Strategies | Strategies | IS-DOES-MEANS for robust actions | Before/after contrast | 350-450 |
| Decisions | Decisions | You-Phrasing, decision triggers | Ratio framing | 250-350 |

**Element-specific polish rules:**

- **Signals:** Ensure signals are weak (emerging, not mainstream). Strengthen contradictory signal acknowledgment. Make uncertainty dimensions explicit. Do NOT upgrade signals to trends.
- **Scenarios:** Verify 2-3 scenarios are truly distinct (not variations of same future). Strengthen plausibility grounding. Ensure implications are explored per scenario.
- **Strategies:** Verify strategies are robust across scenarios (not scenario-specific). Strengthen no-regret move identification. Enhance flexibility/optionality language.
- **Decisions:** Strengthen You-Phrasing ("You decide..." not "The organization should..."). Make decision triggers specific. Include reversibility assessment.

### industry-transformation

| Element | Heading Text | Techniques to Strengthen | Number Play Variant | Word Target |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Hook | (opening paragraphs) | Pyramid Principle | Hero number isolation | 150-200 |
| Forces | Forces | PSB, force quantification | Specific quantification, ratio framing | 350-450 |
| Friction | Friction | Forcing Functions, barrier identification | Before/after contrast | 300-400 |
| Evolution | Evolution | Contrast Structure (current vs. future) | Comparative anchoring | 400-500 |
| Leadership | Leadership | IS-DOES-MEANS, You-Phrasing | Compound impact | 250-350 |

**Element-specific polish rules:**

- **Forces:** Strengthen force magnitude quantification. Ensure multiple force categories present (regulatory, technology, social, economic). Frame forces as irreversible. Strengthen force interaction analysis.
- **Friction:** Strengthen barrier specificity with quantified impact. Identify timing mismatches between forces and friction. Do NOT eliminate friction language — friction is the analytical point.
- **Evolution:** Strengthen contrast between current and future structure. Ensure new equilibrium is described (not just "change"). Quantify power shifts. Include timeline to new equilibrium.
- **Leadership:** Strengthen positioning for the new structure (not defending the old). Apply You-Phrasing. Ensure differentiation sources are specific to the transformed industry.

## Cross-Arc Technique Application

Techniques the copywriter ALWAYS strengthens regardless of arc:

| Technique | How to Strengthen | Where to Apply |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| Number Plays | Replace vague quantities with specific numbers. Apply arc-appropriate variant from table above. | All elements with quantitative claims |
| Power Words | Strengthen verbs (helps → enables, provides → delivers). Apply sparingly (3-5 per element). | Headlines, opening sentences, CTAs |
| Sentence Rhythm | Vary length. Use short punch after long setup. | Transitions, element openings/closings |
| You-Phrasing | Convert third-person to direct address where the element's technique map calls for it. | Elements marked with You-Phrasing above |
| Citation Density | Verify 15-25 total citations across full narrative. Do NOT remove any. | All elements |

## Technique Validation Checklist

After polishing, verify each element still contains its required techniques:

```
FOR EACH element IN arc:
  1. Heading text unchanged? (MANDATORY)
  2. Primary technique intact? (e.g., PSB for Why Change, Forcing Functions for Why Now)
  3. Number Play variant applied? (check table above)
  4. Word count within target range? (±50 words tolerance)
  5. Citations preserved? (count ≥ original)
  6. Element serves its distinct purpose? (no blending between elements)
```

If any check fails, revert that element to its original text and log the failure.
